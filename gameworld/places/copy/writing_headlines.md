# Copy Writing — Pain-Point Headlines That Convert

## The Pain-Point Framework

Structure: **[Their Frustration] = [Your Solution]**

```
Stop [problem] without [side effect]
[Problem] is holding you back from [outcome]
The gap between [current state] and [desired state]
```

## Examples by Industry

### SaaS / Analytics
- "Your data is a mess. We fix that."
- "Stop drowning in dashboards. Start making decisions."
- "From spreadsheet chaos to clarity in one afternoon."

### Fashion / Lifestyle
- "You have too many clothes. None of them feel like you."
- "Getting dressed shouldn't feel like a compromise."
- "The closet you actually want to open."

### Tech / Product
- "Building in public sounds great until you're embarrassed to ship."
- "Your side project deserves a launch that matches its potential."
- "Stop using tools that fight you."

### Finance / Consulting
- "The ROI conversation your CFO actually wants to have."
- "Strategy without execution is just a PowerPoint."
- "Your competitive advantage, quantified."

## CTA Formulas

| Type | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Primary | [Action] + [Specific Outcome] | "Start your free trial" |
| Secondary | [Value] + [Low Friction] | "See how it works" |
| Urgency | [Time-sensitive] + [Benefit] | "Lock in your rate" |

## Social Proof Placement

1. **Hero section** — "Trusted by X companies"
2. **After first CTA** — Quick testimonial
3. **Feature sections** — Relevant quote per feature
4. **Before final CTA** — Detailed case study snippet

## Common Mistakes

- **Feature dumps** — Listing what you do instead of what they get
- **Vague benefits** — "empowering", "revolutionary", "best-in-class"
- **No urgency** — Nothing compels action today
- **Wall of text** — Visual hierarchy lost in paragraphs
